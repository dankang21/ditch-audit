## 1. What this project is

The inferential chain from "physical reality has an external cause" to Nicene
Christian theism has been discussed piecewise (gap problem; ramified natural
theology; dwindling probabilities) but never measured as a whole. We individuate
the chain into eight witness-certified steps and audit 20 years of peer-reviewed
philosophy of religion, coding each article's step, direction, claim strength, and
load-bearing epistemic type, then joining venue tier. Contribution type:
measurement of the dialectic, not participation in it. The project takes no
position on any step's truth.

## 2. The eight steps (compressed; full table = outline §3)

S1 external cause of physical reality · S2 that cause is a personal agent ·
S3 unique, a se, uncreated (pantokrator) · S4 perfectly good · S5 special action
post-creation (miracles, prayer, self-disclosure) · S6 Hebrew prophetic tradition
as the authentic revelation bearer · S7 bodily resurrection of Jesus ·
S8 resurrection ⇒ incarnation + Trinity.

Individuation criterion: a step is admitted iff (i) logically necessary for the
Nicene-minimal target, (ii) not derivable from prior steps — certified by a
**witness**: an extant scholarly position accepting all prior steps and rejecting
this one, (iii) a dedicated peer-reviewed literature disputes it specifically.
Key witnesses: Bostrom's simulator (S3), Draper's indifference (S4), Wiles (S5),
Islam/Judaism (S6), Lapide (S8 — accepts S1–S7).

## 3. Hypotheses (coder-facing contexts must never see these)

- **H1 (type–status law, article level).** Among mainstream-venue items at CS4–CS5:
  pro-direction items are predominantly a priori (alpha); contra-direction items
  predominantly empirical/historical (beta/gamma). Registered prediction: OR ≥ 3.
- **H2 (venue topography).** Among pro items, P(CS4+ | confessional venue) >
  P(CS4+ | mainstream venue); contra items as control (predicted null/reversed).
- Derived step-status (contested/headwind/frozen) is computed from aggregates
  (outline §4.4), never coded per article.
