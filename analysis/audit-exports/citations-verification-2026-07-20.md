# Verification memo — v0.5 citation and witness completions (2026-07-20)

Six source-verification exercises run for the v0.5 repair cycle (second external review).

## 1. Lapide quotation — corrected source layer
The sentence "I accept the resurrection of Easter Sunday not as an invention of the community
of disciples, but as a historical event" appears at **p. 15 of Lapide (1983, Augsburg)** —
inside Carl E. Braaten's introduction, introduced by "In Lapide's own words:", and echoed on
the volume's back cover. Archive.org full-text search of the 1983 scan finds the sentence
nowhere in Lapide's own chapters, and finds no hit at p. 92 (a circulating citation, rejected).
Wording notes: "a historical event" (not "an historical"); the "resurrection of Jesus …"
variant is the 2002 Wipf & Stock back-cover blurb. The paper cites it as Braaten's
introduction quoting Lapide.

## 2. Lessing "ugly, broad ditch" — p. 55, comma confirmed
Chadwick (1957, Stanford) p. 55; Chadwick re-quotes it in his own introduction at p. 31; the
1956 A. & C. Black printing paginates identically. Full sentence: "That, then, is the ugly,
broad ditch which I cannot get across, however often and however earnestly I have tried to
make the leap."

## 3. Maimonides, Hilkhot Melakhim 11:4 (uncensored) — citable edition fixed
Touger translation (Moznaim, 1987), which restores the censored 11:4 passage on Jesus of
Nazareth from Yemenite manuscripts and early uncensored printings (translator's note confirms
the restoration; text verified at Chabad.org's hosted edition). Yale/Hershman 1949 could not
be confirmed to carry the uncensored passage and was not used.

## 4. Qur'an 33:40 — translation fixed
Abdel Haleem (Oxford University Press, 2004; corrected reissue 2008). Verbatim: "he is God's
Messenger and the seal of the prophets" — the phrase quoted in the paper's witness note.

## 5. Plantinga (2006) "partial concession" — quasi-verified via same-issue rejoinder
Primary PDF paywalled; the same-issue rejoinder (McGrew and McGrew 2006, Philosophia Christi
8(1), 23–38; publisher-typeset PDF from the author's site) quotes Plantinga 2006 with pages:
p. 12 ("No such general refutation was proposed or envisaged"), p. 17 (concedes he "cannot
see clearly how" the dwindling-probabilities principle illuminates the case), pp. 19, 21
(re-appraisal of historical apologists; "delighted" at a strong argument). The McGrews'
characterization: "a welcome retreat … But it *is* a retreat" (p. 24); summary at p. 37 notes
the core position is retained. The paper reports the characterization as the exchange's other
party's, with these pages.

## 6. Carnley — S7 stance-witness verification (basis of the v0.5 Table 1 addition)
Candidates screened: Carnley (FITS), Küng (MISFIT — rejects intervention against laws of
nature, S5), Schillebeeckx (UNCLEAR — affirms a real resurrection of Jesus under CDF
examination; coding risk), Borg (MISFIT — rejects supernatural theism and intervention
language, S5/S2), Wedderburn (MISFIT — agnosticism extends beyond S7; prior-step acceptance
not established). Carnley (Anglican Archbishop of Perth 1981–2005; Primate 2000–2005): *The
Structure of Resurrection Belief* (Clarendon, 1987) and *Resurrection in Retrospect: A Critical Examination of the Theology of N. T. Wright* (Cascade, 2019; ISBN 978-1-5326-6751-0) hold Nicene theism, present special divine action (Spirit epistemology), and the
scriptural tradition fixed while denying that the Easter event can be established historically
and arguing, from Paul's "spiritual body," against a material-bodily construal; resurrection
faith rests on present acquaintance with the living Christ. The Sydney evangelical boycott of
his primatial installation over precisely this denial corroborates the S1–S6-yes / S7-no
structure. Mode note: his S5 acceptance runs through the Spirit's present activity, not a
general apologetic of nature-miracles.
