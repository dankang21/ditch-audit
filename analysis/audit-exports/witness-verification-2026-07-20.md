## [PARTIAL] Bostrom, simulation argument (2003, Philosophical Quarterly 53(211): 243-255) — S3 (unique/self-existent/uncreated ground)

**Evidence.** Verified against the author's posted text of the paper (simulation-argument.com/simulation.html). (1) Within-branch structure DOES match the assigned role: under disjunct (3), the simulators are intentional external creators of our world — 'the posthumans running a simulation are like gods in relation to the people inhabiting the simulation: the posthumans created the world we see; they are of superior intelligence; they are "omnipotent"... and they are "omniscient"...' — while S3 is positively denied, not merely suspended: simulators are non-unique ('Reality may thus contain many levels'; 'all the demigods except those at the fundamental level of reality are subject to sanctions by the more powerful gods living at lower levels'), dependent and possibly themselves created ('we would have to suspect that the posthumans running our simulation are themselves simulated beings'), and non-transcendent ('all the elements of such a system can be naturalistic, even physical'). (2) BUT Bostrom does not accept S1-S2: his published position is only the trilemma plus the recommendation 'it seems sensible to apportion one's credence roughly evenly between (1), (2), and (3)' — i.e., he suspends over the entire S1∧S2∧¬S3 package rather than accepting S1-S2 and balking at S3. This also rules out FITS-AS-SUSPENDER, which would still require acceptance of the prior steps. (3) Structural caveat on S1: the hypothesis supplies an external cause only for the simulated world; the hierarchy 'bottoms out' at an uncaused basement level, so S1 is accepted only under a restricted 'our observed universe' reading and is effectively denied for physical reality as a totality. Weakened roles available: (a) structural/conditional witness showing the logical independence of S3 from S1-S2 (an intentional external creator need not be unique, self-existent, or uncreated); (b) pair with published assertoric uptake, e.g., Steinhart 2010, 'Theological Implications of the Simulation Argument,' Ars Disputandi 10: 23-37, which develops Bostrom's 'naturalistic theogony' and finds the SA 'less friendly' to theism.

**Sources.** Bostrom 2003, author's text at simulation-argument.com/simulation.html (verbatim quotes); Steinhart 2010, Ars Disputandi 10: 23-37 (PhilPapers rec STETIO-9; Tandfonline DOI 10.1080/15665399.2010.10820012)

**Disposition wording (basis of the paper's note).** Bostrom's (2003) simulation argument is coded as a structural rather than assertoric witness at S3: conditional on its third disjunct, our world has an intentional external creator (S1-S2 satisfied for the simulated world) that is nonetheless non-unique, dependent, and possibly itself created — a direct denial of S3. Two qualifications apply. First, Bostrom asserts only the trilemma and recommends apportioning credence 'roughly evenly' across its disjuncts, so he accepts neither S1 nor S2 assertorically. Second, the hypothesis grounds only the simulated world, leaving basement-level reality uncaused; it therefore satisfies S1 only under a restricted 'our observed universe' reading. We cite it as demonstrating the logical independence of S3 from S1-S2, not as an endorsed stopping point (for assertoric theological uptake see Steinhart 2010).

## [FITS] Russell, brute-fact position (Russell-Copleston BBC debate, broadcast 28 Jan 1948; repr. in Russell, Why I Am Not a Christian, 1957, and Hick ed., The Existence of God, 1964) — S1 (external cause/ground of physical reality)

**Evidence.** Verified against the full transcript (ditext.com/russell/debate.html) and SEP 'Cosmological Argument' §5.2 ('Objection 1: The Universe Just Is'). S1 is the first step, so the prior-step acceptance condition is vacuous and the fit turns entirely on rejection/suspension of S1 itself. Russell rejects S1 on two published grounds: (i) meta-level illegitimacy of the demand — 'the concept of cause is not applicable to the total,' affirming 'Yes, that's my position' when asked whether asking for the cause of the world is illegitimate, backed by the composition diagnosis ('Every man who exists has a mother, and it seems to me your argument is that therefore the human race must have a mother, but obviously the human race hasn't a mother'); and (ii) first-order denial of any ground — 'I see no reason whatsoever to suppose that the total has any cause whatsoever' and 'I shouldn't say unintelligible — I think it is without explanation... the universe is just there, and that's all.' Rejection-vs-suspension: his in-debate self-description 'my position is agnostic' concerns God's existence globally, not S1; on S1 specifically he goes beyond suspension to denying both the explanatory demand and any known cause, so he codes as a rejecter (with the nuance that part of the rejection is semantic — the question is ill-formed for the totality — rather than an assertoric 'the universe is uncaused'). Publication status: transcript reprinted in standard scholarly venues; SEP itself cites the Hick 1964 reprint ('Russell 1948 [1964]: 175') and characterizes Russell as denying that the universe needs an explanation. Minor venue note: origin is a broadcast debate transcript, not a peer-reviewed article, if venue coding matters downstream.

**Sources.** Transcript: ditext.com/russell/debate.html; SEP, 'Cosmological Argument,' §5.2 (cites Russell 1948 [1964]: 175); Wikipedia, 'Copleston-Russell debate' (broadcast date and reprint history)

**Disposition wording (basis of the paper's note).** Russell (in Russell and Copleston 1948; repr. Hick 1964) is a full witness at S1: he both denies that the totality of physical reality has any cause ('I see no reason whatsoever to suppose that the total has any cause whatsoever'; 'the universe is just there, and that's all') and rejects the explanatory demand as ill-formed when applied to the whole ('the concept of cause is not applicable to the total'), diagnosing the contrary inference as a fallacy of composition. His professed agnosticism in the debate concerns God's existence, not the brute-fact stance; at S1 his position functions as rejection rather than suspension, though part of its force is semantic (the question is ill-posed for the totality) rather than a first-order assertion that the universe is uncaused.

## [FITS] Maurice Wiles, God's Action in the World (Bampton Lectures, SCM 1986) — S5

**Evidence.** Rejection of S5 confirmed and it is denial, not suspension: Wiles holds that the whole of creation is God's one intentional act and that there are no particular divine interventions post-creation — biblical miracle-stories are mistaken or symbolic, and petitionary prayer is reconceived non-interventionistically (publisher description, SCM Press; Themelios review; Wikipedia summary of the 1986 Bamptons). Prior-step acceptance: S1 and S2 are explicit (the world as a whole is a single creative ACT of God — agency and intention are constitutive of his position); S4 is not merely accepted but load-bearing — his central argument is that an omnibenevolent God who intervened selectively (Cana but not Auschwitz/Hiroshima) would be arbitrary and immoral, so he sacrifices S5 precisely to preserve S4; S3 is undisputed-standard for a creedal Anglican (priest, Regius Professor of Divinity, Oxford 1970–91) with no published rejection, though it is acceptance-by-default rather than explicit defense. His skepticism about incarnation doctrine (contributor to The Myth of God Incarnate, 1977) concerns S7/S8 and is irrelevant to the S5 witness role, which requires only S1–S4. His position is a published, extensively engaged scholarly stance — Keith Ward's Divine Action (1990) is a book-length reply treating Wiles as the canonical no-special-divine-action theist. One optional footnote: critics (Ward among them) argue his God is functionally deistic, but deism itself accepts S1–S4, so the objection does not threaten the witness assignment; it corroborates it (the draft already pairs 'Wiles; deism' in the cell).

**Sources.** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maurice_Wiles ; https://scmpress.hymnsam.co.uk/books/9780334020288/gods-action-in-the-world ; https://www.thegospelcoalition.org/themelios/review/gods-action-in-the-world/ ; https://peped.org/philosophicalinvestigations/extract-6-maurice-wiles-miracles-as-immoral-acts/ (Ward, Divine Action, 1990 reply)

**Disposition wording (basis of the paper's note).** Wiles (1986) certifies the S4/S5 boundary from inside theism: he affirms the world as the single intentional act of a good creator — holding S1–S4 fixed — while denying that God ever acts specially within it, rejecting miracles and interventionist answers to prayer on the ground that selective intervention would impugn the very goodness affirmed at S4.

## [PARTIAL] Paul Draper, 'Pain and Pleasure: An Evidential Problem for Theists' (Noûs 23, 1989) — Hypothesis of Indifference — S4

**Evidence.** Person-level, Draper fails the witness criterion: he is a published agnostic — 'Seeking But Not Believing: Confessions of a Practicing Agnostic' (in Howard-Snyder & Moser eds., Divine Hiddenness, CUP 2002, 197–214) — who suspends judgment on theism wholesale, i.e., on S1–S3 as well as S4; he does not 'accept all prior steps.' Hypothesis-level, HI as defined also does not accept S1–S3: 'neither the nature nor the condition of sentient beings on earth is the result of benevolent or malevolent actions performed by nonhuman persons' (Draper 1989, 13, quoted in SEP 'The Problem of Evil') is explicitly neutral between naturalism and supernaturalism — it is a disjunction covering no-creator worlds and indifferent-creator worlds alike. What DOES instantiate accept-S1–S3-reject-S4 is the restricted supernaturalist disjunct Draper canvasses — the indifferent-deity variant (an omnipotent, omniscient creator with no concern for the suffering or pleasure of other beings; see SEP's presentation) — and, in Draper's later work, aesthetic-deism-style comparisons. Weakened role available: the S4 boundary is certified not by a stance-taker who stops there but by a seriously defended, peer-reviewed epistemic possibility of (S1–S3 ∧ ¬S4); this still does the non-derivability work of witness condition (2), since non-derivability needs the conjunction to be a live position in logical space, but it is weaker than the intro's rhetoric of 'where someone in the literature actually stops.' Note the same qualification already silently applies to the cell's co-witness Law's evil-god (Law is an atheist; the challenge is parity-style), and Mill's finite god is the nearest person-level occupant but Mill's designer works on pre-existing materials, which strains S3 — so the S4 cell as a whole should be flagged as hypothesis-level in the text. Draper 1989's own conclusion is comparative-probabilistic (a prima facie epistemic problem for theists), not an endorsement of any indifferent creator.

**Sources.** https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/evil/ (HI definition and indifferent-deity variant) ; https://philpapers.org/rec/DRAPAP ; https://philpapers.org/rec/DRASBN ; https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/abs/divine-hiddenness/seeking-but-not-believing-confessions-of-a-practicing-agnostic/2DA323072797E36AD7077F0952B13180

**Disposition wording (basis of the paper's note).** At S4 the certifying positions are hypothesis-level rather than personal stances: Draper's indifference hypothesis (Noûs 1989) — in particular its supernaturalist disjunct, an indifferent creator — and Law's evil-god challenge (2010) each hold the content of S1–S3 fixed while withholding perfect goodness; their authors are, respectively, a professed agnostic (Draper 2002) and an atheist, so the boundary is certified by seriously defended epistemic possibilities of S1–S3 without S4, not by thinkers who themselves stop there.

## [PARTIAL] Charles Hartshorne, Omnipotence and Other Theological Mistakes (SUNY 1984) — dipolar/process theism — S3

**Evidence.** Rejection side fully confirmed (SEP 'Charles Hartshorne,' Dombrowski): the 1984 book's central thesis is that classical omnipotence is a theological mistake — God is 'ideally powerful... given the partial freedom and power of creatures,' not pantokrator; creatio ex nihilo is denied ('both God and the world of creatures exist necessarily, although the details are contingent'), so the universal one-way ontological dependence in S3 fails; and unqualified aseity is rejected — only God's abstract essence is absolute and independent, while God's concrete states depend on creatures (mutual dependence). So he genuinely rejects the S3 conjunction as worded. Two accuracy corrections, though: (a) Hartshorne does NOT reject divine uniqueness or uncreatedness — his God is unique, uncreated, and necessarily existent (he is the century's chief defender of the modal ontological argument) — so the draft's phrase 'the aseity–uniqueness package' (05_formal.md) misdescribes him; the rejected package is aseity–omnipotence–creatio-ex-nihilo. (b) Prior-step acceptance is not complete as S1 is worded: S2 is fully accepted (a personal God of 'steadfast benevolence,' purposive and responsive), but S1's 'external cause or ground' is only accepted on the 'ground' disjunct and in qualified form — panentheism places the world IN God ('all is in the one God,' SEP) rather than external to God, and God everlastingly grounds cosmic order without originating the world's existence from nothing (some world or other exists necessarily). He is on the non-brute-fact side of S1's contrast with Russell/Oppy, but not an 'external cause' theorist. Weakened role: intra-theistic boundary witness certifying that the classical attribute package is not derivable from grounded personal theism — exactly the role §8's reply to the Thomist objection (O2) needs — provided S1-acceptance is glossed via its 'ground' disjunct and the cell's co-witnesses (Bostrom's simulator, Mormon finitism) carry the strict external-cause reading.

**Sources.** https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/hartshorne/

**Disposition wording (basis of the paper's note).** Hartshorne (1984) certifies the S3 boundary from within theism under a qualified reading of S1: he affirms a unique, uncreated, necessarily existent personal God who everlastingly grounds the world's order (S1 on its 'ground' disjunct — his panentheism qualifies 'external,' and he denies creatio ex nihilo) and full intentional agency (S2), while rejecting the classical package of unqualified aseity and pantokrator omnipotence; the cell's remaining witnesses carry the strict external-cause reading. [Also amend 05_formal.md: 'aseity–uniqueness package' → 'aseity–omnipotence package,' since Hartshorne affirms uniqueness and uncreatedness.]

## [MISFIT] Judaism — S6

**Evidence.** Under the current Table 1 wording ('The authentic bearer of that God's special revelation is the Hebrew prophetic tradition'), Judaism AFFIRMS S6 rather than rejecting or suspending it: the revelatory authenticity of the Hebrew prophetic tradition is constitutive of rabbinic Judaism (Maimonides' principles of faith 6-8: prophecy, Mosaic prophecy, Torah from heaven). Judaism accepts S1-S5 (classical Jewish theism: external cause, personal agent, unicity/aseity, perfect goodness, miracles and providence), so the prior-step condition is met; the failure is directional - there is nothing at this boundary for Judaism to decline. What Judaism does reject, in a dedicated published literature logically independent of and prior to the resurrection question, is the CHRISTOLOGICAL CONSTRUAL of the prophetic corpus: (a) Rembaum, 'The Development of a Jewish Exegetical Tradition Regarding Isaiah 53,' Harvard Theological Review 75/3 (1982) 289-311, documents the Jewish exegetical line reading the suffering servant as Israel, against the christological-predictive reading; (b) Berger, The Jewish-Christian Debate in the High Middle Ages: A Critical Edition of the Nizzahon Vetus (JPS, 1979), is a critical edition of a medieval corpus of Jewish refutations of christological prooftexts from the Hebrew Bible; (c) Maimonides, Mishneh Torah, Hilkhot Melakhim 11:4 (uncensored text), rejects Jesus as the figure of whom 'all the prophets spoke' on the ground that he failed the prophetic criteria (temple, ingathering) - a rejection of prophecy-fulfillment, stated centuries before any modern historiography of the resurrection. This is rejection, not suspension. Therefore the assignment is salvageable only by re-wording S6 to include the christological reading as a conjunct. If S6 is kept as bare revelatory authenticity of the tradition, Judaism must be dropped from S6; its natural station is S7, where a named published Jewish witness exists: Geza Vermes, The Resurrection: History and Myth (Penguin, 2008), accepts Israelite theism and examines the evidence yet rejects bodily resurrection in favor of visionary explanations (and note the paper already uses Lapide as the Jewish theist who UNIQUELY accepts S7, which presupposes that standard Jewish positions stop at S7, not S6). Additional rigor note: 'Judaism' as a corporate entity does not strictly satisfy the paper's own witness definition ('an extant scholarly position... with dedicated peer-reviewed literature'); the table should cite named articulations (e.g., Maimonides Hilkhot Melakhim 11:4; Berger 1979; Rembaum 1982).

**Sources.** Rembaum, HTR 75 (1982): https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/harvard-theological-review/article/abs/development-of-a-jewish-exegetical-tradition-regarding-isaiah-53/C2893055CD8872F705FCFA053A709BB3 ; Berger 1979 critical edition: https://philpapers.org/rec/COHTJD ; Maimonides, Laws of Kings 11 (Chabad trans.): https://www.chabad.org/library/article_cdo/aid/1188356/jewish/Melachim-uMilchamot-Chapter-11.htm and https://www.jewsforjudaism.org/knowledge/articles/maimonides-laws-pertaining-messiah/ ; Vermes 2008 (S7 alternative): https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/2253634 and https://www.patheos.com/blogs/kermitzarleyblog/2015/05/review-of-geza-vermes-book-the-resurrection/

**Disposition wording (basis of the paper's note).** Option A - re-word S6 so Judaism fits (recommended if both witnesses are to be kept at S6): 'S6: That God's special revelation is authentically borne by the Hebrew prophetic tradition as received in its canonical scriptures - reliably preserved, unsuperseded by any later revelation, and rightly read as projecting the messianic career that the Christian claim presupposes (the christological construal of the prophetic corpus).' Add footnote: 'Rabbinic Judaism accepts S1-S5 and the tradition's bare revelatory authenticity; it certifies the S6 boundary by rejecting the christological construal (Maimonides, Hilkhot Melakhim 11:4; the polemical corpus edited in Berger 1979; the exegetical tradition on Isaiah 53 documented in Rembaum 1982) - a rejection logically independent of any verdict on S7. Islam certifies the same boundary by rejecting the preservation and non-supersession conjuncts (see S6 note on tahrif and finality). That the two witnesses reject different conjuncts is itself grain-information: under the ten-step variant (R1), S6 splits into S6a (the received text is the preserved, unsuperseded bearer) and S6b (the corpus rightly read anticipates the Christian messianic claim), each independently witness-certified.' Option B - keep S6 as bare authenticity-of-the-tradition: then delete Judaism from the S6 witness cell (it affirms the step) and, if a Jewish witness is wanted downstream, add Vermes (2008) at S7.

## [FITS] Islam — S6

**Evidence.** Rejection, not suspension - but the wording of S6 must be sharpened for the rejection to bite, and the doctrinal basis should be ordered correctly. Prior steps: Islam accepts S1-S5 - external cause and personal creator (the kalam cosmological argument is of Islamic provenance, al-Ghazali), unicity/aseity/universal dependence (tawhid; al-Qayyum), divine goodness, and post-creation special action including miracles and petitionary prayer (dua). One caveat: Ash'arite voluntarism construes God's goodness non-morally (good = what God commands; al-Ash'ari: 'lying is wrong since God declares it to be wrong'), which qualifies HOW S4 is accepted without denying it - the Mu'tazilite ('adl) and later traditions affirm divine goodness robustly; worth a footnote, not a disqualification. Step direction: Islam affirms the Hebrew prophets' revelatory vocation (the Qur'an affirms the Tawrat, Zabur, Injil as sent down by God), so it does NOT reject S6 read as bare in-origin authenticity of the prophetic tradition. What Islam rejects is that the RECEIVED scriptures are the authentic and FINAL bearer of that revelation, on two published grounds of unequal doctrinal universality: (i) supersession/finality - Muhammad as khatam al-nabiyyin (Q 33:40) with the Qur'an as the final, controlling revelation; the doctrine's history and universality in Sunni Islam are established in Friedmann, 'Finality of Prophethood in Sunni Islam,' Jerusalem Studies in Arabic and Islam 7 (1986) 178-215; (ii) tahrif - but this ground is internally contested: Adang, Muslim Writers on Judaism and the Hebrew Bible (Brill, 1996), shows the majority of classical authorities held tahrif al-ma'na (misinterpretation of an intact text), with only Ibn Hazm and al-Maqdisi asserting textual corruption (tahrif al-nass); Reynolds, 'On the Qur'anic Accusation of Scriptural Falsification (tahrif) and Christian Anti-Jewish Polemic,' JAOS 130/2 (2010) 189-202, argues the Qur'an itself alleges misinterpretation, not textual alteration. Consequence: the paper should NOT rest Islam's witness status on the textual-corruption thesis alone (it is a minority classical position and contested as an exegesis of the Qur'an); the doctrinally universal ground is supersession/finality, with tahrif (in either variant) as reinforcement - even tahrif al-ma'na denies that the tradition as read by Jews and Christians bears the revelation authentically. Note also that Islam's S6 rejection is independent of its downstream denials (Q 4:157 on the crucifixion, rejection of incarnation/trinity), so the witness stands on non-circular grounds at the S6 boundary. Under the sharpened wording proposed for S6 (received text, unsuperseded bearer), Islam is a clean witness; under the current vague wording ('the authentic bearer... is the Hebrew prophetic tradition'), a critic could object that Islam affirms the tradition's authenticity in origin - hence the wording fix is needed here too. As with Judaism, prefer citing named articulations (Ibn Hazm; Adang 1996; Reynolds 2010; Friedmann 1986) over the corporate label to satisfy the paper's own witness definition.

**Sources.** Friedmann, JSAI 7 (1986): https://www.academia.edu/17120349/Finality_of_prophethood_in_Sunni_Islam ; Adang 1996 (Brill): https://brill.com/display/title/1676 and IJMES review https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/international-journal-of-middle-east-studies/article/abs/146EAE49D12A87F047A1097195BC94A8 ; Reynolds, JAOS 130 (2010): https://philpapers.org/rec/REYOTQ (PDF: https://almuslih.org/wp-content/uploads/Library/Reynolds,%20G%20-%20The%20Quranic%20accusation.pdf) ; Seal of the Prophets doctrine survey: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seal_of_the_Prophets ; Ash'arite divine command caveat: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ash'arism

**Disposition wording (basis of the paper's note).** For the S6 witness cell and its note: 'Islam certifies the S6 boundary by rejection, not suspension: it accepts S1-S5 and affirms the Hebrew prophets' revelatory vocation, while denying that the received canonical scriptures are the authentic and unsuperseded bearer of that God's special revelation. The load-bearing ground is supersession: the Qur'an as final revelation and Muhammad as seal of the prophets (Q 33:40; Friedmann 1986), a doctrine universal across classical schools. The tahrif charge reinforces the rejection but is internally differentiated - textual corruption in Ibn Hazm (Adang 1996), misinterpretation of an intact text on the majority classical view and arguably in the Qur'an itself (Reynolds 2010) - and either variant suffices to deny that the tradition as received bears the revelation authentically. Islam's rejection at S6 is independent of its downstream denials at S7-S8 (Q 4:157), so the certification is non-circular. A footnote should register that Ash'arite meta-ethics construes the divine goodness of S4 voluntaristically; this qualifies the mode, not the fact, of Islam's acceptance of the prior steps.' This requires the S6 thesis itself to name the received text and non-supersession, e.g. the Option-A wording proposed under the Judaism entry (conjuncts: preserved canonical text; unsuperseded; christologically construed), of which Islam rejects the first two conjuncts and Judaism the third.

**Disposition wording (basis of the paper's note).** Lüdemann (1994) rejects S7 on historical-critical grounds, explaining the Easter appearances as grief-generated subjective visions and holding that Jesus' body decayed. We therefore cite him not as a witness who accepts S1–S6 while rejecting S7, but as step-contestation literature: published evidence that S7 is professionally contested within critical biblical scholarship on grounds that do not presuppose acceptance of the prior chain.

## [FITS-AS-SUSPENDER] Allison, Dale C., Jr. (2021). The Resurrection of Jesus: Apologetics, Polemics, History — S7

**Evidence.** 유보(거부 아님) 확인: 책의 결론적 판정은 증거 미결정성 — 'the purely historical evidence is not, on my view, so good as to make disbelief unreasonable, and it is not so bad as to make faith untenable' (리뷰들에서 반복 인용). 빈 무덤은 '개연적으로 비어 있었다'고 보되 그것과 신학적 부활 주장의 연결은 역사가로서 유보 ('I think the tomb was probably empty, but I'm not sure how to connect it'); 부활체의 형이상학적/존재론적 지위 판정을 명시적으로 거절 (p. 260, Harriman 리뷰); 양 진영 모두에 대해 'neither side can do what it claims to have done'. 선행 스텝 수용: 자기서술상 실천하는 (리버럴) 기독교인 — 'I am a Christian... I'm also an historian, and I'm also a doubter' (Bible for Normal People ep. 206); 매일 기도, 교회 출석, 유물론 거부('everything can be reduced to chemicals and electricity'라는 세계관 거부), 종교적 경험·특별 행위 가능성에 공개적으로 열려 있음 — S1–S5 수용/개방 문서화됨; S6은 명시적 논증보다 신앙 실천에 함축된 수용. 중요한 한정 두 가지: (1) 그의 유보는 2차적(역사적 증거가 무엇을 보증하는가에 대한 유보)이며, 1인칭 신앙으로는 부활을 긍정 — 'I believe that the disciples saw Jesus and that he saw them, and next Easter will find me in church' (p. 3). 즉 '증거 기반 확립'에 대한 유보이지 명제 자체의 부정이 아님 — 이는 오히려 suspender 범주의 모범 형태임. (2) 'non liquet'라는 라틴어 표현 자체는 2021년 책의 원문 인용으로 웹에서 검증하지 못함(리뷰·인터뷰 모두 미사용) — 논문에서 인용부호 없이 특성화 표현으로 쓰거나 지면 확인 필요.

**Disposition wording (basis of the paper's note).** Allison (2021) functions as a suspension witness for S7: a self-described practicing Christian who affirms theism and is explicitly open to special divine action, yet whose published verdict on the historical question is deliberately indecisive — the historical evidence, he concludes, 'is not so good as to make disbelief unreasonable, and it is not so bad as to make faith untenable' — and who declines to adjudicate the ontological status of the risen body. Two qualifications apply: his suspension is second-order, targeting what unaided historical evidence warrants rather than the proposition itself, which he continues to affirm as a matter of personal faith; and the label non liquet is our characterization of this verdict, not a quotation.

## [FITS] Lapide, Pinchas (1983). The Resurrection of Jesus: A Jewish Perspective (trans. W. Linss; German orig. 1977) — S8

**Evidence.** 모범 사례로 확인됨. S1–S6: 정통파 유대교 신학자로서 이스라엘의 하나님(고전적 유일신론 — S1–S4), 하나님의 기적적 행위(S5), 히브리 계시 전통(S6)을 신앙고백적으로 수용 — Kesher Journal이 '정통파 유대인'으로 확인. S7 수용: 'I accept the resurrection of Jesus not as an invention of the community of disciples, but as a historical event' — 부활을 하나님의 실제 행위/역사적 사건으로 긍정하며, 제자들의 변화에 대한 가장 현실적인 설명으로 육체적 부활을 논증. S8 거부(유보 아님): Time(1979) — 'He flatly denies that Jesus, resurrected or not, was either the Messiah of Israel or the divine Son of God.' 유대 메시아 기대에 메시아의 부활이 포함되지 않으므로 부활은 예수가 이스라엘의 메시아임을 지시하지 않는다고 논증; 성육신 불수용('does not claim to embrace Jesus as Messiah or as God incarnate'); 삼위일체 거부는 별도 출판물(Lapide & Moltmann, Jewish Monotheism and Christian Trinitarian Doctrine, 1981)에서도 문서화. 대신 부활을 이방인을 이스라엘의 하나님께로 이끄는 신적 섭리(praeparatio messianica, 마이모니데스 인용)로 해석. 즉 S7까지 전부 수용하면서 S8의 추론(부활→성육신+삼위일체)을 정면으로 차단하는 출판된 학술적 입장 — 사슬-증인 기준을 완전 충족. 부수 한정 하나: S1–S4 수용은 철학적 논증이 아닌 신앙고백적 수용이며, 메시아 문제를 종말론적으로 열어두는 수사(최종적 판별은 도래 시점에)가 있으나 부활로부터의 기독론적 추론 거부 자체는 명시적이고 일관됨.

**Sources.** https://time.com/archive/6854303/religion-resurrection/ ; https://www.kesherjournal.com/article/the-resurrection-of-jesus-another-jewish-perspective/ ; https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/987202 ; Lapide & Moltmann (1981), Jewish Monotheism and Christian Trinitarian Doctrine

**Disposition wording (basis of the paper's note).** Lapide (1983) is the paradigm S8 witness: an Orthodox Jewish theologian who accepts the God of Israel (S1–S4), divine miraculous action (S5), the Hebrew revelatory tradition (S6), and the resurrection of Jesus as a real historical act of God (S7) — 'not as an invention of the community of disciples, but as a historical event' — while flatly denying that this event shows Jesus to be Israel's Messiah, God incarnate, or a person of a triune God (S8; cf. Lapide and Moltmann 1981 on the Trinity). He reads the resurrection instead as divine providence preparing the gentile world for the God of Israel, thereby severing precisely the inferential link that S8 asserts.
